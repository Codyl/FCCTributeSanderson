1975

Born in Lincoln Nebraska. Sanderson had been a fantasy obsessive since the age of 14, when a copy of Dragonsbane by Barbara Hambly turned the reluctant reader into a lifetime fan.

1994

Enrolled at BYU-I in biochemestry. publishers kept telling him that his epic fantasies were too long, that he should try being darker or “more like George RR Martin” (it was the late 90s, and A Song of Ice and Fire was topping bestseller charts). His attempts to write grittier books were terrible, he says, so he became “kind of depressed”.

1995

Served as a missionary for the Church in Seol. Brandon often says that it was during this time in Seoul, Korea that he realized that he didn’t miss chemistry one bit, but he did miss writing.

2003

A publisher, Tor, finally showed interest in one of his novels: Elantris, an epic fantasy set in a former city of the gods, now devoid of magic. Tor was also interested in The Way of Kings, but Sanderson, in an unusual move, decided “something wasn’t quite right” and pulled it.

2005

Published his first novel called Elantris.

2010

Published the way of kings.

2014

Words of Radiance, landed Sanderson his first No 1 on the New York Times bestseller charts.
